This course focuses on designing efficient approximation algorithms for large-scale streaming data with approximation guarantees. Although the pre-requisite for this course is CS3230, I feel that they are not very closely related (maybe loosely related in the sense of graphs like spanning trees which are also taught in CS2040). I would recommend taking this course after taking ST2131 or ST2334 because analysing performance guarantee requires some familiarity with probability. One of the top courses I've taken as an undergrad. I am not really an algo person, but the motivations for streaming algorithms and how they can be applied in real life are very clear, which make this course extremely eye-opening. Absolutely recommended.
